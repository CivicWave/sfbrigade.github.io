National Day of Civic Hacking - SF 2015

Passionate neighbors, urbanists, civic hackers, developers, designers, government staff, and community organizers from across the globe will come together to improve their communities and governments. Here in San Francisco, we will be focused on tackling challenges and projects in open data, health, safety and justice, economic development, climate, disaster relief, oceans, and mapping. Come to join a project, to test solutions out with residents, to hone skills at workshops, or to just see what is going on.

Shared Prosperity Agenda + National Day of Civic Hacking

In January 2015, Mayor Lee announced the Shared Prosperity Agenda – offering specific activities that the City will undertake for its citizens in the areas of housing, education, transportation, women’s empowerment, and anti-poverty. The National Day of Civic Hacking is a chance for residents to prototype and test out their own solutions in these same issue areas, offering complementary projects to those being created primarily by the Government.

The Details

  • People: 300+ Passionate San Franciscans
  • Skillsets: Developers, Project Managers, Community Organizers, Designers, Researchers, Marketers, Government Staff, Urbanists, etc.
  • Work: Team-based projects focused on civic hacking (improving San Francisco.)
  • What to Expect: Participants will join (or bring) data challenges and projects to complete over the weekend. The teams will be 4-10 people and work over 2 days (June 6 & 7). You will hear more about the challenges on Friday night at the opening party and join a team. Saturday and Sunday will be work days, with workshops and fun activities to take a break and meet others. On Sunday afternoon, teams will present their work.
  • Perks: Meet other passionate individuals, work on projects with results, learn new skills in workshops, enjoy complimentary food, play and engage with your city in fun activities.

Schedule

  • Friday, 6/5: Opening Party: speakers, performers, projects recruit team members.
  • Saturday 6/6: Work Day 1: activities, workshops, user testing and project work.
  • Sunday 6/7: Work Day 2: work continues, afternoon presentations, Closing Reception.
