National Day of Civic Hacking - SF 2015

Thousands of people from across the United States will come together to build new solutions to improve our communities and the governments that serve them during the National Day of Civic Hacking. Passionate neighbors, urbanists, civic hackers, developers, designers, government staff, and community organizers will unite around building tools for San Francisco. Come to join a project to tackle specific challenges, to interview residents for user testing, to hone skills at workshops, or to build a stronger SF with your neighbors.

Shared Prosperity Agenda + National Day of Civic Hacking

Earlier this year, Mayor Lee announced the Shared Prosperity Agenda – specific activities that the city will tackle for its citizens in housing, education, transportation, women’s empowerment, and anti-poverty. National Day of Civic Hacking is a chance for citizens to build themselves solutions, empowering communities to build and test out new solutions for these issues.

The Details

  • People: 300+ Passionate San Franciscans
  • Skillsets: Developers, Project Managers, Community Organizers, Designers, Researchers, Marketers, Government Staff, Urbanists, etc.
  • Work: Team-based projects focused on civic hacking (improving San Francisco.)
  • Perks: Meet other passionate individuals, work on projects with results, learn new skills in workshops, enjoy complimentary food, play and engage with your city in fun activities.

Schedule

  • Friday, 6/5: Opening party with speakers and performers; projects recruit team members.
  • Saturday 6/6: Project work starts; activities, workshops, user testing during the day.
  • Sunday 6/7: Work concludes in late afternoon; teams present; closing reception.

Proposed Project Requirements

  • Expectations: We are looking for projects that can be completed in 2 working days by a team of 4 - 10 people.
  • Skillsets: The variety of skills/backgrounds of attendees will include developers, project managers, community organizers, designers, researchers, marketers, government staff, urbanists, and active citizens. It is important that projects are able to use a variety of skillsets and expertise levels, especially given that our attendees come from varied backgrounds.
  • Challenges/Goals: All projects must address a component of how to improve San Franciso. They mush have clearly articulated challenges and tangible goals. They need to be designed for 4 - 10 person teams, with scaleable goals to fit your team's size. The more 'ready for execution' the project, the better.
  • Project Lead: Each project requires a project lead to see the project from start to finish, and attending the 3-day event. If someone cannot attend one of the days, it is okay for two people to collaborative lead the team.
  • Timeline: Friday night: project leads recruit members to their challenge at the Opening Party. Saturday and Sunday: working on projects. Sunday late afternoon: each project presents their work to the group.
