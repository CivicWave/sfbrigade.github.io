National Day of Civic Hacking - SF 2015

Thousands of people from across the United States will come together to build new solutions to improve our communities and the governments that serve them during the National Day of Civic Hacking. Here is San Francisco, passionate neighbors, urbanists, civic hackers, developers, designers, government staff, and community organizers will unite around building tools for San Francisco. Challenges to tackle over the weekend include open data, health, safety and justice, economic development, climate, disaster relief, oceans, and mapping. Come to join a project to tackle specific challenges, to interview residents for user testing, to hone skills at workshops, or to build a stronger SF with your neighbors.

Shared Prosperity Agenda + National Day of Civic Hacking

Earlier this year, San Francisco Mayor Lee announced the Shared Prosperity Agenda – specific activities that the city will tackle for its citizens in housing, education, transportation, women’s empowerment, and anti-poverty. National Day of Civic Hacking is a chance for citizens to build themselves solutions, empowering communities to build and test out new solutions for these issues.

The Details

  • People: 300+ Passionate San Franciscans
  • Skillsets: Developers, Project Managers, Community Organizers, Designers, Researchers, Marketers, Government Staff, Urbanists, etc.
  • Work: Team-based projects focused on civic hacking (improving San Francisco.)
  • What to Expect: Participants will join (or bring) data challenges and projects to complete over the weekend. The teams will be 4-10 people and work over 2 days (June 6 & 7). You will hear more about the challenges on Friday night at the opening party and join a team. Saturday and Sunday will be work days, with workshops and fun activities to take a break and meet others. On Sunday afternoon, teams will present their work.
  • Perks: Meet other passionate individuals, work on projects with results, learn new skills in workshops, enjoy complimentary food, play and engage with your city in fun activities.

Schedule

  • Friday, 6/5: Opening Party: speakers, performers, projects recruit team members.
  • Saturday 6/6: Work Day 1: activities, workshops, user testing and project work.
  • Sunday 6/7: Work Day 2: work continues, afternoon presentations, Closing Reception.

Proposed Project Requirements

  • Focus: Projects that are focused on addressing a component of how to hack our civic space, the city of San Francisco.
  • Skillsets: The variety of skills/backgrounds of attendees will include developers, project managers, community organizers, designers, researchers, marketers, government staff, urbanites, and active citizens.
  • Project Lead: Each project requires a project lead to see the project from start to finish, attending the 3-day event. It is okay for two people to collaboratively lead the team.
  • Goals and Structure: The projects/proposals for the weekend should have tangible goals that can be met by a team of 4-10 people over 2 full days of work. It is important that projects can use varied skills and expertise levels that our attendees office. They mush have clearly articulated challenges and tangible goals – think ‘project in a box’ that is ready for execution. The goals also need to scalable goals to fit a team size between 4 and 10 people.
  • Starting Status: Projects can be at different phases (ideation, testing a specific component, a few steps before launch, etc.) With that, structure the weekend’s project proposal so that new individuals can contribute to a specific goal or milestone.
  • Timeline: Friday night: project leads recruit members to their challenge at the Opening Party. Saturday and Sunday: working on projects. Sunday late afternoon: each project presents their work to the group.
